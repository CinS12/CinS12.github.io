UPC: VR

2022. Specialized course about Mixed Reality (Virtual Reality + Augmented Reality) technologies and applications. Course of the MIRI’s master on Computer Graphics and Virtual Reality specialization. Final project [Unity]: developing an interactive VR experience (headset + handheld controllers) with the following technical aspects:

  • - Personalized navigation methods: physical locomotion (walking) and teleportation; with adjustable settings.
  • - Use of 3D user interfaces, more aproppiate in VR than the regular ones.
  • - Isomorphic selection (laser beam) and manipulation features (attachment and position) for objects and elements.
  • - Constant feedback, through sounds, haptics and visuals such colour changes.
  • - Motion sickness prevention. By ensuring a high and constant frame rate, and contolling the field of view and the speed changes
  • - 360 degrees field of regard. High sense of presence and immersive sensation. The elements are already known for the users in real life. They work and are sized the way it's expected: a key, a door, a house, a brush, etc.

VR: Rehabilitation island

2020. Academic and social VR project. Developed by a team of 4 engineering students with Unreal Engine 4. The goal was to motivate the patients and to monitorize, through gamification, the upper extremities' rehabilitation exercices by using immersive technologies. The application teleports the patients to a tropical island, a calm and relaxed place far from the medical center; where they can play some mini-games. Each game makes the user move such an exercice from the rehabilitation routine specifies; and by doing so, they complete their exercice routine while playing. Finally, to motivate yourself (and the others) and because the fact that the exercices are monitorized through the hand controllers, the obtained score about the quality of the exercies, is put on the scoreboard.

Bachelor Thesis: distinction with honour

2021. Thesis developed in a research internship at La Salle URL, engineering department. The project was about developing a non-invasive tool to assist health personal in the diagnosis and treatment of pressure injuries. The process consists in segmentation of the different tissues that injuries have, from a previously taken image; the creation of a database to storage the segmentation and the metadata of the patient, with the aim of training a classifier system focused on the prediction and diagnosis of these patterns.
